About

Industrial Designer.

Temidayo Abraham Durodola is an industrial designer with a background in automotive engineering. He is focused on developing products that are visually resolved and technically feasible to manufacture. His work spans transportation, consumer products and engineering-led design, with an emphasis on thoughtful decision-making from concept through production.

View Work
Philosophy

Design should earn every decision.

His background in automotive engineering shaped the way he approaches design. Rather than separating aesthetics from engineering, he sees both as parts of the same conversation. Every form influences manufacture, every material affects performance, and every decision carries consequences beyond appearance.

That perspective naturally led him toward industrial design, where creativity is strengthened by technical understanding. His work focuses on navigating the space between concept generation and practical execution, ensuring ideas remain grounded in manufacturing realities while delivering meaningful user experiences.

He believes good products communicate clarity. They should feel intentional, resolve genuine constraints and justify the complexity required to produce them. Whether developing transportation concepts or consumer products, the objective is to create solutions that balance engineering precision with visual restraint.

Successful design is rarely about adding more. It is about making informed decisions, removing unnecessary complexity and allowing every detail to contribute to the whole.
